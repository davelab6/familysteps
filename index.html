My formula describes an Ogee Curve in between the Lucas Theory and Equal steps.
This seems to be the most useful pattern to distribute weight across a font family.
Especially in large families with many weights, or where the Black-Thin difference is lengthy.

You can take this values as a pretty good starting point, and then refine according to your eyes.
Cheers, Pablo Impallari.

John Berry wrote, "The usual progression would be that a jump of two in the weight category would give you a pair of typefaces with sufficient contrast to be used together (one as a bolder equivalent of the other); a jump of only one weight would usually not provide enough contrast to be useful. (For example, you might use Book-Semibold together, or Normal-Bold, but not Normal-Semibold, in this scenario.)"
